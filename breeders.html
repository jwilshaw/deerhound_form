How Breeders Can Help


We require blood samples collected from 45 Deerhounds with dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) and 45 DCM-free, healthy control Deerhounds. We also require their pedigree details, including dam and sire names. Your Deerhound must have received an echocardiogram (cardiac ultrasound) by a vet who should have further cardiology qualifications, such as the RCVS certificate in cardiology, the RCVS Diploma in Veterinary Cardiology, ECVIM or ACVIM Diploma in Cardiology. Ideally, we would also like an ECG and/or a 24 hour Holter although the ECG/Holter are not essential.

To help with the study, please submit information using our online form. Please note that we will require the name of the cardiologist who scanned your Deerhound plus some heart size measurements which can be found on your cardiologistâ€™s echo report. The blood samples can be taken by any vet and will need to be carefully packaged and then posted. Unfortunately, the study cannot cover any postage costs incurred or any fees your vet or cardiologist may charge.
